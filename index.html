Privacy Policy

Screen Time  ·  Effective date: May 27, 2026  ·  Last updated: May 27, 2026

The short version: Screen Time tracks which websites you visit and how long you spend on them. All of this data is stored only on your device, never sent anywhere, and automatically deleted over time. The extension makes zero network requests. There is no account, no server, and no one — including the developer — who can see your data.

What data is collected

Screen Time records the domain name of each website you visit (e.g. youtube.com) and the number of seconds your browser's active, focused tab was open on that domain.

That is the entirety of what is tracked. The extension does not record:

  • Full URLs, page titles, or any page content
  • Your search queries or anything you type
  • Scroll position, clicks, mouse movements, or keystrokes
  • Your IP address or geographic location
  • Any personally identifiable information
  • Browsing activity in incognito windows

Where data is stored

All data is stored using Chrome's chrome.storage.local API — a sandboxed database that exists entirely on your local machine inside Chrome's profile directory. It is:

  • Never synced to Google's servers or any other server
  • Not accessible to any website or other browser extension
  • Permanently and automatically deleted when you uninstall Screen Time

How long data is kept

Data Detail level Retention
Today's browsing Per site, per second Until midnight, then archived
This week's days Per site, per second Until Sunday, then compressed
Past weekly summaries Category totals only — individual site names discarded Up to 12 weeks, then dropped

After a week ends, the names of individual websites you visited that week are permanently deleted. Only the total time per category (e.g. "4 hours on Video") is retained. Your long-term history never reveals which specific sites you visited.

Network requests

Screen Time makes zero network requests. The extension contains no fetch, XMLHttpRequest, or WebSocket calls. It cannot communicate with any external server under any circumstances. You can verify this yourself by opening Chrome DevTools on the extension's service worker and inspecting the Network tab — it will be empty.

Third-party sharing

Your data is never sold, shared, rented, or disclosed to any third party. There is no analytics service, no crash reporting tool, and no advertising SDK embedded in this extension.

Permissions used

Screen Time requests only the permissions necessary for it to function:

  • tabs — to detect which tab is active and read its URL domain
  • storage — to save and retrieve your time data locally
  • alarms — to schedule the daily midnight reset and periodic data flush
  • webNavigation — to detect when you navigate to a new page within an active tab

No other permissions are requested or used.

Questions

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or how Screen Time handles your data, you can reach out via the Chrome Web Store support page for this extension.
